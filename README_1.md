# Accountability by Design

**A Proposal-Grade Work Breakdown Framework for Creative Practice**

> Built in collaboration with AI. Directed entirely by human judgment.

---

## What This Is

Most creative projects fail quietly — not from lack of talent, but from lack of structure. **Accountability by Design** applies proposal-writing discipline to creative project management, translating every deliverable into a formally defined framework across six fields grounded in GCA Government Contracting Academy methodology.

This tool is for practitioners who want to think about their work the way government proposal writers think about contracts — with rigor, accountability, and zero tolerance for vague scope.

---

## The Framework

Every deliverable is defined across six fields:

| Field | What It Surfaces |
|---|---|
| **Understanding** | What does the client actually need? |
| **Solution Compliance** | Does your approach meet every stated and implied requirement? |
| **Benefit** | What does the client gain? What measurable outcome? |
| **Proof Point** | What evidence proves you can deliver? |
| **Gap to Watch** | Known unknowns, dependencies, vulnerabilities |
| **Estimated Time** | Hours, honest |

---

## Features

- **Project Information** — Name, client, practitioner, type, objective, win themes, known gaps
- **4-Perspective Template** — GCA-grounded Understanding → Compliance → Benefit → Proof Point per deliverable
- **Go / No-Go Self-Scoring** — 19-category, 57-point rubric grounded in GCA bootcamp methodology with live score and decision output
- **Printable Output** — Clean, structured breakdown document printable to PDF via browser
- **Tabbed Interface** — Project Info → Deliverables → Go/No-Go → Generate

---

## Methodology

Grounded in:
- **GCA Government Contracting Academy** — RFP Go/No-Go Self-Scoring Rubric & Proposal Readiness System
- **4-Perspective Template** — Understanding, Solution Compliance, Benefit, Proof Point
- **Capture Management Discipline** — Three V's (Valid, Viable, Value), win themes, discriminators, shred-first methodology
